# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Wanderbound is for travelers who tracked a trip in Polarsteps and want a
polished, print-ready photo album without designing it from scratch. They are
not expected to understand layout or print production. They want the product
to do the heavy lifting while preserving control over the result.

## Product Purpose

Wanderbound turns a Polarsteps export into a strong automatic album with
photos, maps, trip details, and print-aware layouts. Success means a traveler
can upload their data, make only the edits they care about, and export a
print-ready PDF without needing design expertise.

## Positioning

Wanderbound gives travelers ownership of the finished album: it turns
Polarsteps data into an editable, print-ready PDF they can print wherever they
choose, starting from a strong automatic layout rather than a blank canvas.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, personal. The interface makes album creation feel manageable,
earns trust in the generated result, and keeps the traveler's memories at the
center of the experience.

## Anti-references

Wanderbound should not resemble a scrapbook editor, a template marketplace, or
a playful travel app filled with stickers and decorative effects. It should
also avoid the opposite extreme: dense professional design software that
exposes every possible control and makes users construct an album from first
principles.

## Design Principles

1. **Own the finished album**: users control the exported PDF and where it is
   printed.
2. **Start strong, edit selectively**: automatic output should be good enough
   to use, while meaningful refinements remain available.
3. **Memories lead**: photos, maps, and the travel story are the product. The
   interface supports them without competing for attention.
4. **Precision where print depends on it**: layout, image quality, typography,
   and preview behavior should build confidence in the final result.
5. **Practical inclusion**: prioritize accessible core workflows without
   presenting formal conformance as a product promise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG 2.2 AA as guidance when building or revising components, without
claiming formal conformance. Prioritize readable contrast, keyboard access for
core workflows, visible focus, reduced-motion support, and correct RTL and
internationalized behavior. Provide alternatives for essential drag
interactions where practical. Exhaustive remediation of legacy behavior and
user-authored album or print-canvas content is not a project requirement.
