## Design Context

### Users

Travelers who have exported their Polarsteps data and want to turn it into a print-ready photo album — either as a personal keepsake or a gift for a travel companion. They're revisiting memories, not performing a task. The tool should feel like sitting down with a box of postcards, not filling out a form.

### Brand Personality

**Clean, smart, reliable** — with an undercurrent of warmth.

Wanderbound is an engineering-quality tool that handles complexity (GPS tracks, elevation profiles, multi-locale formatting, print layout) so the user doesn't have to. But the surface it presents should feel personal and inviting, not clinical. Think of a well-made journal: precise binding, beautiful paper, but filled with handwritten memories.

**Emotional goal:** Warm nostalgia — the interface should evoke the feeling of flipping through a scrapbook, not operating software.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Reference:** Apple Photos book creation — clean layouts, generous whitespace, photos as hero elements, minimal chrome. Also inspired by Polarsteps' own design: data-rich pages (maps, weather, elevations, flags, countries, step descriptions) are a deliberate feature, not clutter.

**Key tension to resolve:** The album is intentionally metadata-heavy (maps, stats, country silhouettes, elevation profiles, weather). This richness is the product's differentiator. The UI and album design must make this density feel like *plotting and reliving your adventure* — not data entry. Use visual variety, color, spatial rhythm, and contextual presentation to keep rich data feeling alive.

**Theme:** Both dark and light modes are first-class citizens — neither is primary. Both must feel equally polished and intentional.

**Typography:** Inter (body), Heebo (Hebrew supplement), JetBrains Mono (technical displays). Self-hosted, `font-display: block` for PDF reliability.

**Color:** Primary blue `#0063d1`. Semantic stat colors (indigo for days, teal for distance, orange for photos, purple for steps). Dark mode uses deep navy-purple (`#1e1e2e`), light mode uses warm gray (`#f3f4f6`).

**Anti-patterns:**
- Generic "SaaS dashboard" aesthetic (gray everything, no personality)
- Overly playful/illustrative style that undermines the precision of the tool
- Data tables or spreadsheet-like presentation of travel metadata

### Design Principles

1. **Photos are the hero.** Every design decision should maximize the impact of the user's travel photos. The UI, chrome, and metadata exist to support and contextualize the photos — never to compete with them.

2. **Rich data, light touch.** Maps, elevation profiles, weather, and stats are core features — present them with visual craft so they feel like part of the journey, not a dashboard. Color, spatial rhythm, and typography do the heavy lifting.

3. **Print-first album pages.** Album pages are designed for print — avoid `backdrop-filter`, `mask-image`, blend modes, and fixed/sticky positioning (these genuinely break). Shadows and filters work but trigger rasterization. Basic gradients and `clip-path` shapes are fine. See `frontend/CLAUDE.md` for the full reference.

4. **Two styling worlds.** The editor UI (header, sidebar, menus) is designed for screen with themed dark/light modes. Album pages are designed for print — light mode uses pure white backgrounds, colors are chosen for paper. Both dark and light UI modes should feel equally polished. Use CSS custom properties as the single source of truth for all theme-dependent values.

5. **Quiet confidence.** The interface should feel well-made and trustworthy without being loud. Subtle transitions, precise alignment, consistent spacing. Let quality show through craft, not decoration.

### Styling Contexts

**Editor chrome** (header, sidebar, menus, toolbar): Use Quasar utility classes and brand colors. This is the interactive shell.

**Album pages** (cover, overview, photo pages, maps): Use CSS custom properties from `App.vue`. Print-safe only. This is the printable output.

Never mix these contexts — Quasar utility classes on album page elements or `all: unset` conflicting with Quasar classes are both bugs.

### Accessibility

Best-effort: maintain good contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, and ARIA attributes. Support `prefers-reduced-motion`. No formal WCAG compliance target at this stage.
